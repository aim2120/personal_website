A Quick Introduction

Hi! I'm Irene, currently an undergraduate student at Columbia University studying visual arts and computer science and hailing from Seaside, California, on the Central Coast. I currently reside in Morningside Heights in Manhattan, New York, and enjoy painting, coding, reading, perusing the internet, and weightlifting. I currently have a part-time work-study job at my university as security escort to help with expenses and am always on the lookout for additional opportunities, no matter how big or small.

I've made this website as a convenient area to host my artwork and work-related info, as well as a place to post relevant updates to my life and career.

Thanks so much for taking a look around, and feel free to contact me if you want to request a comission, talk about a job opportunity, or simply want to meet and chat sometime!

FAQs

  • How would you describe your art? / What is your artist's statement?
    I am a representational painter who likes to depict the world in a way that seems most accurate to me. Sometimes accuracy comes not from realism, but from emotions evoked. Through my paintings I provide a distorted looking glass that demonstrates the way I view the world. And above all, I value subtle over brash imagery. Take a look at my art to see what I mean.
  • What areas of computer science are you most interested in?
    I have been casually teaching myself web development (HTML, CSS, and JavaScript) since middle school and, with the start of college, have delved more into data storage and programming, primarily with Java. I am looking forward to learning more about graphics, multimedia, and artificial intelligence, but am very open to all possible paths.
  • Are you looking for a more long-term work committment right now?
    I would love to hear about any spaces you might have available! I am available to work part-time during the school year (September–May) and full-time during the summers (June–August). Feel free to download my resume if you'd like some more information about my work history.
  • Are you currently taking commissions?
    Yes! I'm open to creating paintings, drawings, or multimedia projects. I would like to also note that, since I am currently also a full-time student and have a part-time job, we'll have to be flexible with the deadline. Feel free to contact me if you have an idea you'd like to see created. I can give you an estimate after we discuss the idea.
  • Why are you studying two very different majors?
    I have loved art since I was very young and have more recently rekindled an interest in pursuing computer science (I taught myself some basic web dev in middle school), so I couldn't resist studying both in college. I hope to combine the two into a technology-based fine arts career, but am open to all paths and opportunities that present themselves.
