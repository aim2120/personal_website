A Quick Introduction

Hi, I'm Annalise, a visual artist living and practicing in NYC. I focus mainly in oil and acrylic painting, but also have passion in graphic design, videography, and computer programming.

I am in the process of finishing my Bachelor of Arts in visual arts and computer science at Columbia University and plan to continue growing my artistic practices. I enjoy programming too and hope to continue progressing my skills in that field as well.

There are plenty of ways to get in contact with me, so please feel free to reach out if you'd like. I'm open to both art-based and tech-based opportunities.

Sincerely,

Some FAQs

  • Would you like to hear about job openings, internships, or other work commitments?
    Yes, my ear is open to short and long term opportunities in both the creative and tech fields. I am available to work part-time during the school year (September–May) and full-time during the summers (June–August). Feel free to download my resume if you'd like some more information about my work history.
  • Are you currently taking commissions?
    Yes, I'm open to creating paintings, drawings, or multimedia projects. Feel free to contact me if you have an idea you'd like to see created.
  • How would you describe your art? / What is your artist's statement?
    I am a representational painter who likes to depict the world in a way that seems most accurate to me. Sometimes accuracy comes not from realism, but from emotions evoked. Through my paintings I provide a distorted looking glass that demonstrates the way I view the world. Take a look at my art to see what I mean.
  • What areas of computer science are you most interested in?
    I have the most experience in front-end web development, but am most interested in educating myself in the fields of computer graphics and machine learning (image recognition and natural language processing). I'm comfortable in a bash environment and have been mostly self-motivated in my CS pursuits.
  • Are you an artist or a programmer?
    I'm an artist with a deep interest in computer science theory. My artistic practice is my main focus, but wish to continue involvement in areas of tech as well.
