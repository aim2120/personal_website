A Quick Introduction

Hi! I'm Annalise, currently an undergraduate student at Columbia University studying visual arts and computer science. I am originally from Seaside, CA, and reside in Morningside Heights in Manhattan, New York. I enjoy painting, coding, reading, weightlifting, and trying to understand contemporary art. I currently have a part-time work-study job at my university as a security escort to help with expenses, but am also on the lookout for additional opportunities, no matter how big or small.

I made this website as a convenient way to host my artwork, show some code samples, and post relevant updates to my life and career, as well as increase my online presence.

Thanks so much for taking a look around, and feel free to contact me if you want to talk about a job opportunity, request a commission, or simply want to meet and chat sometime!

Sincerely,

Some FAQs

  • Would you like to hear about job openings, internships, or other work commitments?
    I would love to hear about any spaces you have available that I seem the right fit for! I am available to work part-time during the school year (September–May) and full-time during the summers (June–August). Feel free to download my resume from the navigation bar above if you'd like some more information about my work history.
  • Are you currently taking commissions?
    Yes! I'm open to creating paintings, drawings, or multimedia projects. I would like to also note that, since I am currently also a full-time student and have a part-time job, we'll have to be flexible with the deadline. Feel free to contact me if you have an idea you'd like to see created. I can give you an estimate after we discuss the idea.
  • How would you describe your art? / What is your artist's statement?
    I am a representational painter who likes to depict the world in a way that seems most accurate to me. Sometimes accuracy comes not from realism, but from emotions evoked. Through my paintings I provide a distorted looking glass that demonstrates the way I view the world. And above all, I value subtle over brash imagery. Take a look at my art to see what I mean.
  • What areas of computer science are you most interested in?
    I have been casually teaching myself web development (HTML, CSS, and JavaScript) since middle school and, with the start of college, have just started gaining a greater knowledge of programming and data storage, primarily with Java. I have completed a few notable projects and look forward to learning more about graphics, multimedia, and artificial intelligence, but am really just excited about learning more about CS in general.
  • Why are you studying two very different majors?
    I have loved art since I was very young and have more recently rekindled an interest in pursuing computer science (I taught myself some basic web dev in middle school), so I couldn't resist studying both in college. I hope to combine the two into a technology-based fine arts career, but am open to all paths and opportunities that present themselves.
